# Quanter All in Texts (QAT)

**QAT (Quanter All in Texts)** is a research group focusing on the systematic measurement and quantification of textual properties.

The name *Quanter* is a pseudo-French word meaning *quantify*.  
The full name *Quanter All in Texts* is inspired by the expression *conquer all*.

---

## Our Vision

QAT aims to establish principled and standardized ways to measure information in texts.  
We treat texts as structured objects that can be analyzed, modeled, and quantified.

---

## Research Focus

The group studies quantitative measures of linguistic structure and information, including but not limited to:

- Lexical diversity and lexical richness  
- Syntactic complexity and syntactic organization  
- Discourse cohesion and coherence  
- Distributional and information-theoretic properties of texts  

---

## Goal

Our long-term goal is to develop reproducible measurement frameworks for textual analysis and to promote transparent, interpretable, and theory-informed quantification of language data.

---

## Scope

QAT welcomes research from linguistics, quantitative linguistics, computational linguistics, and related fields, with a shared interest in text-based measurement.
